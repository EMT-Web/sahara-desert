8-Day Morocco Imperial Cities & Sahara Desert Tour from Casablanca
By Visit Sahara Desert
Day 1: Casablanca – Rabat – Meknes – Fez
Your private Morocco tour begins with a pickup from your accommodation in Casablanca. The first highlight is a visit to the Hassan II Mosque, the largest mosque in Morocco and the only one open to non-Muslim visitors, beautifully set along the Atlantic coast.
After the visit, we drive north along the coastline to Rabat, Morocco’s capital and one of its four imperial cities. In Rabat, you will explore the Kasbah of the Oudayas, a historic fortress overlooking the Atlantic Ocean, followed by a visit to the Hassan Tower, one of the city’s most iconic landmarks. Nearby, you will also see the Mausoleum of Mohammed V, an architectural masterpiece honoring Morocco’s former kings.
We then continue inland to Meknes, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Morocco’s most fortified imperial cities. Here, you will visit the city’s main highlights, including Bab Mansour, one of the most impressive gates in North Africa, the Qara Prison, Lahdim Square, and the vast Heri es-Souani granaries and stables.
In the late afternoon, we drive to Fez, where you will be dropped off at your traditional riad inside the old medina.
Overnight in Fez.

Day 2: Fez Guided Walking Tour
Today is dedicated to a full-day guided walking tour of Fez el-Bali, one of the world’s oldest and best-preserved medieval cities. Accompanied by an official local guide, you will explore a living museum of Moroccan history and culture.
The tour starts at Bab Bou Jeloud (the Blue Gate), the main entrance to the medina. From there, you will visit Al Quaraouiyine Mosque and University, recognized as the oldest continuously operating university in the world, followed by the beautifully decorated Bou Inania Madrassa.
You will continue through the narrow alleys of the medina souks, home to traditional handicrafts, spices, and artisan workshops. The tour also includes a visit to the famous Chouara Tanneries, where leather has been processed using the same natural methods since medieval times.
At the end of the tour, your guide escorts you back to your riad.
Overnight in Fez.

Day 3: Fez – Ifrane – Midelt – Ziz Valley – Erfoud – Merzouga Desert
After an early departure from Fez, the journey heads south toward the Sahara Desert. The first stop is Ifrane, often called the Switzerland of Morocco, known for its alpine architecture, clean streets, and cool climate.
A short drive leads to the Cedar Forest of Azrou, home to the Barbary macaques, where you can enjoy a brief stop before continuing through the Middle Atlas Mountains.
Lunch is in Midelt, famous for its apple orchards. Afterward, the road follows the dramatic landscapes of the Ziz Valley, filled with palm groves and traditional Berber villages, passing through Errachidia, the gateway to southern Morocco.
You then reach Erfoud, known for its ancient fossils, before arriving at Merzouga and the golden dunes of Erg Chebbi. After a short rest at a desert hotel, you begin a camel ride across the dunes to your desert camp.
Enjoy dinner and a traditional Berber music performance around the campfire under the stars.
Overnight in a Sahara desert camp.

Day 4: Merzouga Desert Excursion
After breakfast at the camp, enjoy a full-day 4x4 desert excursion through the Merzouga region. The day begins with a visit to a nomadic family, where you will share mint tea and learn about desert life and traditions.
You then explore the historic M’fis mines, followed by a visit to Khamlia village, famous for its Gnawa music. Enjoy a live performance before stopping for lunch at a local restaurant known for traditional Berber pizza.
In the afternoon, you visit a seasonal desert lake, an important habitat for flamingos and other migratory birds. Later, return to your hotel near the dunes to relax and enjoy the sunset. Optional activities such as sandboarding or quad biking can be arranged.
Overnight in Merzouga.

Day 5: Merzouga – Rissani – Todgha Gorges – Dades Gorges
Leaving the Sahara behind, the journey continues to Rissani, an important former trading center with lively traditional souks. You will take a walk through the market and experience authentic local life.
Next, drive to Tinghir and the spectacular Todgha Gorges, towering limestone canyons carved by a river flowing from the High Atlas Mountains.
The route then follows the famous Road of a Thousand Kasbahs to the Dades Valley, known for its dramatic rock formations, green fields, and fig and walnut trees.
Overnight in the Dades Gorges.

Day 6: Dades Gorges – Valley of Roses – Skoura – Ouarzazate – Ait Ben Haddou – Marrakech
After breakfast, travel through the Valley of Roses and the Skoura Oasis, stopping to visit the historic Amridil Kasbah, one of Morocco’s best-preserved 17th-century kasbahs.
Continue to Ouarzazate, Morocco’s film capital, where you visit Taourirt Kasbah. A short drive leads to Ait Ben Haddou, a UNESCO-listed fortified village dating back to the 11th century and featured in films such as Gladiator and Game of Thrones.
Cross the High Atlas Mountains via the scenic Tizi n’Tichka Pass (2,260 m) before arriving in Marrakech, where you are dropped off at your riad in the medina.
Overnight in Marrakech.

Day 7: Marrakech Guided City Tour
Your official local guide meets you at your riad for a full-day Marrakech walking tour. Begin at Jemaa el-Fna Square, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its storytellers, musicians, acrobats, and street performers.
Visit the Koutoubia Mosque, the Ben Youssef Madrassa, the elegant Bahia Palace, the ruins of El Badi Palace, and the Saadian Tombs. The tour continues through the vibrant Marrakech souks.
Later, explore the modern part of the city with a visit to the Majorelle Garden and the Yves Saint Laurent Museum. Your guide then escorts you back to your riad.

Day 8: Transfer to Marrakech or Casablanca Airport
Depending on your flight schedule, your driver arranges a timely transfer to Marrakech Airport or Casablanca Airport, marking the end of your unforgettable Morocco journey with Visit Sahara Desert.

15-Day Morocco Imperial Cities, Sahara Desert & Coastal Adventure
Experience the ultimate Morocco adventure with Visit Sahara Desert, exploring imperial cities, the golden Sahara dunes, the Atlas Mountains, and the coastal charm of Essaouira. This 15-day tour combines cultural immersion, camel trekking in Merzouga, sandboarding in Erg Chebbi, visits to Fes medina, Chefchaouen blue city, and the vibrant Marrakech souks, providing an unforgettable journey through Morocco’s history, landscapes, and Berber culture.

Day 1: Arrival – Casablanca to Rabat
Upon arrival at Casablanca Airport, you will be personally greeted and transferred to Casablanca, Morocco’s bustling economic hub. Visit the stunning Hassan II Mosque, the world’s tallest minaret overlooking the Atlantic coastline. Later, you will depart for Rabat, the imperial capital, and check into a riad in the heart of the medina, where you will spend the night surrounded by the city’s history and culture.

Day 2: Rabat – Chefchaouen
After breakfast, explore Rabat, including the iconic Hassan Tower and the ancient medina, where the colorful stalls and artisan shops reflect centuries of Moroccan craftsmanship. We then drive through the Rif Mountains, passing small Berber villages largely untouched by time, arriving in the picturesque blue town of Chefchaouen, perched between two peaks. Overnight in Chefchaouen, also known as Morocco’s “Blue Pearl.”

Day 3: Chefchaouen Sightseeing
Spend the day wandering the labyrinthine streets of Chefchaouen, exploring its Spanish-style squares, lively markets, and vibrant blue-painted houses. The views of the surrounding Rif Mountains are spectacular, and the town provides a unique glimpse into traditional Berber culture.

Day 4: Chefchaouen – Volubilis – Meknes – Fes
Today, journey to the ancient Roman ruins of Volubilis, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Explore the Forum, Basilica, Caracalla Arch, and stunning mosaics that showcase Morocco’s historical depth. Continue to Meknes, often called the “Versailles of Morocco,” visiting the grand Bab el Mansour gate, Moulay Ismail Mausoleum, and the historic city walls. In the afternoon, arrive in Fes, the intellectual and spiritual capital of Morocco, for dinner and overnight stay.

Day 5: Fes Medina Guided Tour
Discover Fes el Bali, the oldest car-free urban area in the world. This guided tour includes visits to the Royal Palace gates, Mellah (Jewish Quarter), Chouara Tanneries, Moulay Idriss Mausoleum, and Al Qaraouine University, considered the world’s oldest continuously operating university. End the day with a visit to a local tile cooperative for panoramic views over Fes’ medina, immersing yourself in the city’s centuries-old culture.

Day 6: Fes – Ifrane – Azrou – Midelt
Depart Fes to the charming Alpine-style town of Ifrane, known as Moroccan Switzerland. Continue through the Middle Atlas Mountains, exploring Azrou cedar forests where Barbary macaques live in their natural habitat. Enjoy the scenic mountain landscapes before arriving in Midelt, where you will spend the night in a comfortable hotel surrounded by the Atlas Mountains.

Day 7: Midelt – Ziz Valley – Erfoud – Merzouga – Camel Trek
Travel along the lush Ziz Valley, dotted with palm groves, fortified villages, and ancient kasbahs. Stop in the desert town of Erfoud, an oasis known for fossils, before arriving in Merzouga. Here, mount your camels and embark on a Sahara camel trek into the pink Erg Chebbi dunes, arriving at a traditional Berber desert camp. Enjoy a night under a sky full of stars with authentic Moroccan cuisine and music.

Day 8: Merzouga – Ramlia Village & Sandboarding
Wake up to the breathtaking sunrise over the Erg Chebbi dunes. Today, try sandboarding down the tallest dunes in the Sahara. Visit the remote Berber village of Ramlia, meet the local people, and explore the Ramlia Children’s Literacy Fund, a community education initiative. Spend the night in a local Berber guesthouse for an immersive cultural experience.

Day 9: Ramlia – Alnif – Todra Gorge – Dades Valley
Depart Ramlia, crossing the striking black desert and palm-filled oases. Stop in Alnif, renowned for its fossil trade. Continue to the Todra Gorge, with its sheer 160-meter cliffs, often called the “Grand Canyon of Morocco.” End the day in the Dades Valley, surrounded by red-orange cliffs, kasbahs, and spectacular scenery.

Day 10: Dades – Ouarzazate – Ait Benhaddou – Marrakech
Travel along the famous Road of a Thousand Kasbahs, admiring ancient fortresses. Discover Ouarzazate, the “Hollywood of Morocco,” home to famous film studios that produced blockbusters such as Gladiator and Alexander the Great. Visit Ait Benhaddou, a UNESCO World Heritage site and iconic film location. Continue across the High Atlas Mountains to Marrakech, Morocco’s legendary Red City, for overnight stay.

Day 11: Marrakech – Essaouira
Travel through valleys filled with argan, olive, and orange groves on the way to the Atlantic coast. Explore the medina of Essaouira, an ancient whitewashed town with Portuguese influence, wandering its alleys and lively harbor where fresh seafood is abundant. Enjoy a walk or horseback ride along the pristine Atlantic beaches before overnighting in Essaouira.

Day 12: Essaouira – Marrakech
Visit a women’s argan oil cooperative to see traditional extraction methods of this world-famous product. Return to Marrakech and explore the vibrant Djemaa el Fna Square, where street performers, acrobats, and food stalls create a truly unforgettable atmosphere.

Day 13: Marrakech Sightseeing
Enjoy a guided tour of Marrakech, visiting landmarks such as the Majorelle Gardens, Koutoubia Mosque, Saadian Tombs, and Bahia Palace. Experience the lively souks and medina, practice bargaining skills, and immerse yourself in the heart of Moroccan culture.

Day 14: Free Day in Marrakech
Spend a free day in Marrakech at your leisure. Optional activities include a Moroccan cooking class or additional exploration of the city’s historic sites, gardens, and hidden corners.

Day 15: Marrakech Airport Transfer
On your final day, a private transfer will take you to Marrakech Airport, ensuring you arrive on time for your departure, concluding your Morocco desert and imperial cities adventure.
