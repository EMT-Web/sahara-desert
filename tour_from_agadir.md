Tours from Agadir
3 Days Sahara Desert Tour from Marrakech
Itinerary
Day 1: Agadir → Tinghir → Ouarzazate → Dades Gorges
Your real desert adventure begins with a pick-up from your hotel in Agadir around 7:30 AM after breakfast. The journey takes you through stunning landscapes dotted with Berber villages, kasbahs, and lush oases. Your first stop will be Taliouine, known as the capital of saffron, where the famous saffron flower is cultivated. Next, you will visit the UNESCO World Heritage site of Ait Benhaddou Kasbah, a magnificent fortified village that has served as the backdrop for numerous films. The day ends as you arrive at the Dades Gorges, where you will spend the night surrounded by dramatic cliffs and scenic landscapes.
Day 2: Dades Gorges → Todgha → Merzouga
After breakfast and a hot shower at the guesthouse, you will continue your journey towards Merzouga. Along the way, you will experience the stunning Todgha Gorges, known for its striking limestone canyons. Upon arrival in Merzouga, the highlight of the day awaits: a camel trek into the spectacular Erg Chebbi sand dunes, where you can witness the desert sunset. Be sure to wake up early the next morning if you wish to catch the breathtaking sunrise in the heart of the Sahara Desert. The night is spent in a desert tent under a sky full of stars, enjoying traditional Berber cuisine and music.
Day 3: Merzouga → Agadir
The final day of the tour is mainly dedicated to the return journey to Agadir. There will be multiple stops along the way for rest, meals, and photo opportunities, allowing you to soak in the diverse landscapes of Morocco one last time. The tour usually concludes around 9 PM, with the option for a free drop-off at Agadir Airport instead of returning to your hotel.

4-Day Morocco Sahara Desert Tour from Agadir to Marrakech
Day 1: Agadir → Taznakht → Ait Ben Haddou → Dades Valley
Begin your Visit Sahara Desert adventure with an early departure from Agadir, heading towards the breathtaking Dades Valley. Along the way, stop in Taznakht, a charming Berber town famous for its vibrant handwoven carpets and traditional crafts. Continue to the iconic Ait Ben Haddou, a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of Morocco’s most famous kasbahs. Wander its ancient alleyways and admire the traditional mud-brick architecture that has been featured in films such as Gladiator and Lawrence of Arabia. Drive through the stunning High Atlas Mountains, where dramatic landscapes and panoramic views unfold at every turn. Arrive in the Dades Valley, known for its dramatic gorges and verdant oases, and settle into your riad or hotel to relax and prepare for the journey ahead.
Day 2: Dades Valley → Todra Gorge → Erfoud → Merzouga
After a hearty breakfast, explore the spectacular Dades Gorges, dotted with ancient Berber kasbahs and villages, offering a glimpse into Morocco’s traditional way of life. Travel through the valley of a thousand kasbahs, passing charming settlements and lush palm groves as you head toward Tinghir. Continue to the awe-inspiring Todra Gorge, a natural wonder with towering cliffs rising up to 300 meters. Stop for lunch before reaching Erfoud, renowned for its fossils and artisan workshops. Arrive in Merzouga, the gateway to the Sahara Desert, where your camel caravan awaits. Embark on a magical camel trek into the golden Erg Chebbi dunes, witnessing the desert sunset before settling in a traditional Berber camp. Enjoy a hearty Moroccan dinner under a sky filled with stars, experiencing the serenity and magic of the Sahara.
Day 3: Merzouga Desert → Nomad Village → Khamlia → Rissani → Ouarzazate
Wake up early to experience the unforgettable sunrise over the Erg Chebbi dunes, a highlight of any Sahara Desert tour. After breakfast, explore the desert further with optional camel rides, sandboarding, or a visit to local nomad families to discover their unique lifestyle. Visit Khamlia village, known as the “Village of the Black People,” where traditional Gnawa music fills the air. Later, travel through Rissani, a historical caravan town with bustling markets and authentic Moroccan charm. Continue through the Draa Valley, with its endless palm groves and ancient kasbahs, arriving in Ouarzazate, Morocco’s cinematic city, in the late afternoon. Spend the night in a local riad or hotel, reflecting on the breathtaking landscapes of the Moroccan desert and Atlas Mountains.
Day 4: Ouarzazate → High Atlas Mountains → Marrakech
After breakfast, explore Ouarzazate, including its impressive kasbahs and historical landmarks. Learn about the region’s culture, architecture, and history before departing for Marrakech. Cross the dramatic High Atlas Mountains via the scenic Tizi n’Tichka pass, stopping to take in sweeping views of the valleys and Berber villages. On the way, revisit the remarkable Ait Ben Haddou, where you can take more time to admire the UNESCO-listed fortress and capture stunning photographs. Continue the journey to Marrakech, arriving in the heart of the medina by the evening. This marks the end of your 4-Day Morocco Sahara Desert tour from Agadir to Marrakech, leaving you with unforgettable memories of deserts, kasbahs, and the majestic Atlas Mountains.

5 Days Visit Sahara Desert Tour from Agadir
Itinerary
Day 1: Agadir → Marrakech
Begin your 5-day Sahara Desert tour with a pick-up from your hotel in Agadir and a scenic drive to the vibrant city of Marrakech. Upon arrival, immerse yourself in the rich Moroccan culture and history with a guided tour of the city. Explore the bustling Jemaa el-Fnaa square, alive with street performers, food stalls, and merchants, creating a truly authentic Moroccan experience. Visit the iconic Koutoubia Mosque, famous for its soaring minaret, and enjoy the lively streets of Marrakech. Spend the night in a comfortable riad or hotel, enjoying the charm and hospitality of Marrakech.
Day 2: Marrakech → Atlas Mountains → Ait Ben Haddou → Skoura → Dades Gorges
After breakfast, depart Marrakech and cross the High Atlas Mountains via the breathtaking Tizi n’Tichka pass, offering panoramic views of snow-capped peaks, valleys, and Berber villages. Visit the historic Ait Ben Haddou, a UNESCO World Heritage site, renowned for its well-preserved kasbahs and as a backdrop for famous films. Continue through the scenic Skoura Oasis and arrive at the stunning Dades Gorges, where dramatic cliffs, red rock formations, and lush palm groves create unforgettable scenery. Overnight in a cozy hotel or guesthouse in Dades Valley.
Day 3: Dades Gorges → Todra Gorge → Erfoud → Merzouga
Drive through the captivating Dades Valley in the morning, then head to the impressive Todra Gorge, a natural wonder with sheer cliffs rising over 160 meters, popular with hikers and climbers. Continue your journey towards Merzouga, the gateway to the Sahara Desert, where you will embark on a camel trek into the golden Erg Chebbi dunes. Witness a breathtaking desert sunset and spend the night at a traditional Berber desert camp, enjoying Moroccan cuisine and local music under a star-filled sky.
Day 4: Merzouga Desert Tour → Alnif → Tazarin → Ouarzazate
Spend the morning exploring the magnificent Merzouga Desert, with activities including camel riding, sandboarding, and trekking through the Erg Chebbi dunes, some of the tallest in the Sahara. Visit Khamlia village and interact with local nomad families, experiencing traditional desert life. In the afternoon, depart Merzouga and travel through Rissani, famous for its bustling local market, before arriving in Ouarzazate, known as the "Hollywood of Morocco," where you will see traditional architecture and desert landscapes. Overnight in Ouarzazate.
Day 5: Ouarzazate → Taznakht → Taroudant → Agadir
On your final day, depart Ouarzazate and drive through the scenic landscapes of southern Morocco to Taznakht, a town celebrated for its exquisite Berber carpets and handicrafts. Explore local markets and workshops to witness the artistry of traditional weaving. Continue your journey through the picturesque landscapes of Taroudant and finally return to Agadir by late afternoon. Reflect on your unforgettable 5-day adventure, from the bustling souks of Marrakech to the serene Sahara Desert, dramatic gorges, and charming Berber villages, marking the end of a truly immersive Moroccan tour.

7-Day Morocco Sahara Desert Tour from Agadir (Return to Agadir)
Day 1: Agadir → Essaouira
Your Visit Sahara Desert adventure begins in Agadir, where we pick you up from your hotel or the airport and head north along Morocco’s scenic Atlantic coast. The first stop is the tranquil beach of Sidi Kaouki, perfect for a leisurely lunch and to soak in the ocean breeze. Continuing to the historic coastal city of Essaouira, you will explore its whitewashed medina, ancient ramparts, and bustling fishing port. Essaouira is a UNESCO-listed town renowned for its charm, artisan shops, and vibrant atmosphere. Spend the night in a comfortable riad or hotel, enjoying a relaxing Moroccan-style evening.
Day 2: Essaouira → Marrakech
After breakfast, depart Essaouira for the energetic city of Marrakech, a highlight on any Sahara Desert tour from Agadir. Meet your local guide for an immersive exploration of the city’s treasures. Wander through the intricate alleys of the medina, browse the colorful souks, and marvel at the architectural splendor of the Bahia Palace and Saadian Tombs. End your guided tour with a stroll through Jemaa el-Fnaa, the legendary square alive with performers, storytellers, and traditional Moroccan flavors. Overnight stay in Marrakech, where you can enjoy the city’s vibrant night ambience.
Day 3: Marrakech → Ait Ben Haddou → Valley of Roses → Dades Gorges
Today, you leave Marrakech and cross the majestic High Atlas Mountains via the panoramic Tizi n’Tichka pass, offering breathtaking views of rugged valleys and Berber villages. Stop at the iconic Ait Ben Haddou, a UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for its fortified kasbahs and as a backdrop for films such as Gladiator and Game of Thrones. Continue through the fragrant Valley of Roses and the charming Berber village of Kalaat M’Gouna before arriving at the dramatic Dades Gorges. Spend the night in a local riad or hotel, surrounded by the towering red cliffs and peaceful desert landscapes.
Day 4: Dades Gorges → Todra Gorge → Merzouga (Sahara Desert)
After breakfast, drive through the awe-inspiring Todra Gorge, where the vertical cliffs create a natural wonder attracting climbers and photographers alike. Continue your journey through Erfoud, known for its fossil-rich landscape and traditional handicrafts. Arrive in Merzouga, the gateway to the Sahara Desert, and embark on a camel trek into the mesmerizing Erg Chebbi dunes. Witness a golden Saharan sunset before settling at a luxury Berber desert camp. Enjoy a traditional Moroccan dinner under a blanket of stars, experiencing the serene beauty of the desert night.
Day 5: Merzouga → Rissani → Draa Valley → Ouarzazate
Wake early to watch the sunrise over the dunes, a magical moment on any Sahara Desert tour. After breakfast, explore the historic town of Rissani, a former caravan trading post with a vibrant market. Continue through the lush Draa Valley, dotted with palm groves and ancient kasbahs. Arrive in Ouarzazate, Morocco’s “Hollywood of the Desert,” and explore the Taourirt Kasbah. Overnight stay at a riad or hotel, reflecting on the diverse landscapes you have experienced, from endless desert dunes to fertile oases.
Day 6: Ouarzazate → Taroudant → Agadir
Depart Ouarzazate and journey to Taroudant, often called “Marrakech’s grandmother,” known for its fortified walls and bustling souks. Visit local markets and traditional workshops, especially for Berber carpets and handicrafts. Continue to Taznakht, a town famed for its artisan weaving. Arrive in Agadir by late afternoon and check into your hotel or riad, where you can relax and reflect on the incredible Sahara Desert tours and cultural experiences of your journey.
Day 7: Free Day in Agadir
Enjoy a leisurely breakfast before a full day at your own pace in Agadir. Relax on the golden beaches, explore the local markets, or take optional excursions to nearby attractions. Agadir offers plenty of activities, from watersports to cultural experiences, making it the perfect end to your 7-day Sahara Desert adventure from Agadir. Overnight stay at your hotel.
